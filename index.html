2. How CRISP Works (User Manual)

The 3 Core Frameworks

HOAC-II

The 7-step clinical reasoning algorithm. CRISP guides you through identifying existing problems, forming hypotheses, and testing strategies.

ICF

The biopsychosocial model used to frame patient problems, goals, and outcomes across body functions, activities, and participation.

KNGF

Dutch Evidence-Based Guidelines. CRISP utilizes these to ensure you learn standardized diagnostic and therapeutic methods.

Feedback & Socratic Coaching

CRISP doesn't give answers; it asks questions. Based on your inputs, it will adopt one of three feedback modes:

🚩

Interrupt

CRISP stops you immediately if you miss a Red Flag or make a critical reasoning error that compromises patient safety.

🤔

Challenge

CRISP will ask "Why?" or "What else?" to push you to justify your clinical choices and reveal gaps in your logic.

👍

Coach

When you're stuck, CRISP provides hints, scaffolds the next step, or gives examples to help you find the way forward.

✖ What CRISP Will NOT Do

  • • Give answers without effort: It will not write your assignment or solve the case for you.
  • • Diagnose real patients: It is strictly for simulated educational scenarios.
  • • Replace lecturers: Always consult human supervisors for final clinical judgments.

⚠ Important System Traits

  • • Message limits: Free ChatGPT accounts have daily caps on complex GPT messages. Keep prompts focused.
  • • No persistent memory: Starting a new chat wipes all context. Always save summaries.
  • • No automatic grading: Your lecturer cannot see your chat logs unless you share them yourself.
💾 How to Save Your Session

To continue your reasoning later, use this prompt at the end of your session:

"Summarise our progress so I can copy the text and resume this case next time."

Note: Copy the resulting text into a private document (Word/Notion/Notes).
