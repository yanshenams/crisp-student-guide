📚 The Case Study Library: Standard vs. Longitudinal

CRISP features a library containing both standard cases and five specific longitudinal cases (Cases 1-5).

Why Longitudinal? Patients at different stages of their life or recovery present with different types of symptoms. It can be highly misleading for students if there is an error in assessing the timeframe. A standard case would be much easier for students to solve, but these longitudinal scenarios teach teachers how to use all the evolving case information to teach properly and challenge students' dynamic reasoning.

Common User Manual

The 3 Core Frameworks

HOAC-II

The 7-step clinical reasoning algorithm. CRISP guides you through identifying existing problems, forming hypotheses, and testing strategies.

ICF

The biopsychosocial model used to frame patient problems, goals, and outcomes across body functions, activities, and participation.

KNGF

Dutch Evidence-Based Guidelines. CRISP utilizes these to ensure you learn standardized diagnostic and therapeutic methods.

✖ CRISP Guardrails

  • Will not give answers without reasoning.
  • Will not diagnose real patients.
  • Will not bypass lecturer supervision.

⚠️ Important System Traits

  • • Message limits: Free ChatGPT accounts have daily caps on complex GPT messages. Keep prompts focused.
  • • No persistent memory: Starting a new chat wipes all context. Always save summaries.
  • • No automatic grading: Your lecturer cannot see your chat logs unless you share them yourself.
